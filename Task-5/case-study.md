# Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud

**Overview:**
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a growing concern for students in India. This crime involves tricking victims into authorizing payments to a scammer’s account under the guise of receiving money.

**How it Typically Happens:**
1. **The Trap:** A scammer contacts a student via a marketplace (like OLX) or social media, claiming they want to buy an item or provide a "scholarship" prize.
2. **The Deception:** The scammer sends a QR code or a "Payment Request" link via apps like Google Pay or PhonePe.
3. **The Trick:** They tell the student, "Scan this to receive your money." In reality, scanning the code and entering a UPI PIN *sends* money rather than receiving it.
4. **The Theft:** Once the student enters their PIN, the funds are instantly transferred to the scammer's account, which is often a "mule account" to hide their identity.

**Target & Consequences:**
College students are often targeted due to their frequent use of digital payments and interest in part-time earnings. The consequences range from immediate financial loss to emotional distress and potential identity theft if personal details were also shared during the interaction.
